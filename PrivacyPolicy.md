# Studere — Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** March 28, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 28, 2026

---

## Summary

Studere is a local-first application. Your research data stays on your device. We do not collect, transmit, store, or sell your personal information or study design data.

---

## 1. Our Core Commitment

Studere was built with a foundational principle: **research data is sensitive and should remain under the researcher's control at all times.**

Study protocols may contain information about vulnerable populations, proprietary interventions, institutional strategies, unpublished hypotheses, and pre-registration details that could compromise research integrity if disclosed prematurely. Studere is designed so that none of this data ever leaves your Mac.

---

## 2. Data That Stays on Your Device

The following data is created and stored exclusively on your local machine using Apple's SwiftData framework:

- **Research projects** — titles, creation dates, modification dates, and status
- **Study components** — all nodes in your study design graph, including component types, titles, and position data
- **Inspector answers** — every response you provide to the Socratic Inspector questions (inclusion criteria, intervention descriptions, outcome measures, sample sizes, rationale, and all other fields)
- **Relationships** — the methodological connections between your study components
- **AI-generated drafts** — all protocol prose generated by the on-device language model
- **User preferences** — your selected model tier and any other app settings

This data is persisted in your local SwiftData store, typically located within your app's container directory. It is not synced to iCloud, not backed up to any external server operated by us, and not accessible to anyone other than you and your operating system's standard backup processes (e.g., Time Machine).

---

## 3. On-Device AI Processing

Studere uses open-source language models running locally on your Mac via Apple's MLX framework. All AI inference — including model loading, prompt construction, and text generation — happens entirely on your device.

### Model Downloads

When you first use the protocol drafting feature, Studere downloads a pre-quantized language model from HuggingFace (huggingface.co). This is the **only network request** the app initiates, and it transfers only the model weights — no user data is sent. Specifically:

- **What is downloaded:** A publicly available, open-source language model (e.g., Mistral 7B Instruct, Qwen3 4B, or Qwen2.5 14B Instruct, depending on your selected tier)
- **What is NOT sent:** Your project data, inspector answers, study designs, protocol drafts, or any other user-generated content
- **When it happens:** Only on first use of a given model tier, or if the cached model is deleted
- **Where it is stored:** In a local cache directory on your Mac, managed by the MLX framework

After the initial download, the model runs entirely offline. You can verify this by disconnecting from the internet after the model is cached — protocol generation will continue to work.

### Prompt Construction

When generating a protocol draft, Studere constructs prompts from your inspector data and sends them to the local model. These prompts are:

- Built in-memory from your SwiftData store
- Passed directly to the local MLX inference engine
- Never written to disk in prompt form (only the final generated output is retained)
- Never transmitted over any network connection

---

## 4. Data We Do Not Collect

To be explicit, Studere does **not** collect or process:

- Personal identifying information (name, email, institution, affiliation)
- Usage analytics or telemetry
- Crash reports (unless you choose to share them through macOS system-level crash reporting, which is controlled by your macOS settings and handled by Apple, not by us)
- Study content or research data of any kind
- Device identifiers or hardware fingerprints
- Location data
- IP addresses or network information
- Browsing or interaction patterns within the app

We have no servers, no databases, no user accounts, and no backend infrastructure that receives data from the app.

---

## 5. Third-Party Services

Studere interacts with one external service:

| Service | Purpose | Data Sent | When |
|---|---|---|---|
| HuggingFace (huggingface.co) | Download open-source language model weights | Standard HTTP request (no user data) | First use of a model tier only |

HuggingFace's own privacy policy governs their handling of download requests. We encourage you to review it at [huggingface.co/privacy](https://huggingface.co/privacy).

Studere does not integrate with any advertising networks, analytics platforms, social media SDKs, or cloud AI services (such as OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, or others).

---

## 6. Data Export

When you export a protocol draft or structured data from Studere, the exported file is saved to a location you choose on your local file system via macOS's standard file save dialog. We have no visibility into where you save these files or what you do with them afterward.

You are responsible for the handling, sharing, and storage of any exported files.

---

## 7. Data Retention and Deletion

Since all data is stored locally on your device:

- **Your data exists for as long as you keep it.** Studere does not impose retention periods or automatic deletion.
- **To delete a single project:** Use the delete function within the app. This removes the project and all associated nodes, edges, and inspector data from your local SwiftData store.
- **To delete all app data:** Uninstall Studere from your Mac. This removes the app container, including all stored projects and cached models.
- **To delete cached models only:** Remove the MLX model cache directory from your local file system.

We cannot delete your data because we do not have it.

---

## 8. Security

Studere relies on the security measures provided by macOS and your local environment:

- **App Sandbox:** Studere runs within macOS's app sandbox, limiting its access to the file system and other apps.
- **SwiftData encryption:** Your data is stored using Apple's SwiftData framework, which benefits from the encryption and access control mechanisms of your Mac's file system (FileVault, if enabled).
- **No network exposure:** Because no user data is transmitted over the network, there is no risk of interception, man-in-the-middle attacks, or server-side data breaches related to your research data.

We recommend enabling FileVault on your Mac and using a strong login password, particularly if your research involves sensitive or regulated data.

---

## 9. Children's Privacy

Studere is a professional research tool and is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or the applicable age of digital consent in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect information from children, as we do not collect information from anyone.

---

## 10. Regulatory Considerations

Because Studere does not collect, process, or transmit personal data:

- **GDPR (EU):** No personal data processing occurs. There is no data controller or data processor relationship between us and you with respect to your research data.
- **HIPAA (US):** Studere does not create, receive, maintain, or transmit protected health information (PHI) on behalf of any covered entity. If your research involves PHI, you are responsible for ensuring that the data you enter into Studere on your local device is handled in accordance with your institution's HIPAA policies.
- **CCPA/CPRA (California):** We do not sell or share personal information, as we do not collect it.

Researchers working with regulated data (clinical trials, patient information, institutional data) should consult their institutional review board (IRB), data protection officer, or compliance office regarding local device use policies.

---

## 11. Changes to This Policy

If we update this privacy policy, we will revise the "Last Updated" date at the top and include the updated policy in the next release of the app. Because Studere does not collect contact information, we cannot notify you directly of changes — we recommend reviewing this policy when you update the app.

Our commitment to local-first, no-collection architecture is a design principle, not just a policy position. Any future changes that would alter this architecture would be communicated clearly and would require your affirmative action (such as opting into a cloud feature).
